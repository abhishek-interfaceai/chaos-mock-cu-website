Early Withdrawal Penalty

Certificates that are closed before maturity are subject to an early withdrawal penalty. For terms of 12 months or less, the penalty is 90 days of dividends. For terms longer than 12 months, the penalty is 180 days of dividends. The Flex Certificate allows one penalty-free withdrawal during its term.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when my certificate matures?
Your certificate will automatically renew for the same term at the current rate unless you take action during the 10-day grace period after maturity. You can withdraw funds, change terms, or add additional deposits during this grace period without penalty.
Can I add funds to an existing certificate?
Standard certificates do not allow additional deposits after opening. However, the 18-Month Flex Certificate allows one additional deposit of at least $250 during the term. You can also add funds during the grace period at renewal.
Are certificates NCUA insured?
Yes, all deposits at Greenfield Federal Credit Union, including certificates, are federally insured by the National Credit Union Administration (NCUA) up to $250,000 per individual depositor.
What is the difference between APY and dividend rate?
The dividend rate is the simple interest rate paid on your deposit. The APY (Annual Percentage Yield) reflects the total amount of dividends you will earn over a year, taking into account the effect of compounding. The APY is always slightly higher than the dividend rate because it includes the benefit of compound interest.
